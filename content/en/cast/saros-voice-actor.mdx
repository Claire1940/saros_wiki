## The Challenges of Modern Game Acting

Acting for a title like *Saros* presents challenges that differ wildly from traditional film or television. Actors must often perform in "the volume," wearing tight suits covered in reflective markers and head-mounted cameras. There are no sets, no costumes, and often no co-stars present in the room. The **saros voice actor** must rely entirely on their imagination to visualize the alien landscapes and high-tech corridors of the game world.

Furthermore, the non-linear nature of game development means that scenes are frequently recorded out of order. An actor might record the ending of the game in their first week and the introductory tutorial months later. To combat this, Housemarque provided Kohli with extensive "sides" (script excerpts) and overarching summaries to keep the character’s evolution consistent.
