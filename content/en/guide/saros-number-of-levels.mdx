## Progression and Fast Travel Mechanics

One of the most significant changes in Saros compared to its predecessors is how it handles the **saros number of levels** during a single play session. Housemarque has listened to player feedback regarding long, exhausting runs. In Saros, once you have successfully cleared a biome and reached the next one, you unlock the ability to start future runs directly from that furthest point.

This "Fast Travel" system ensures that your play sessions remain focused and manageable, typically lasting between 15 to 20 minutes. However, there is a tactical tradeoff to consider. If you skip earlier biomes, you arrive at the later levels with fewer weapon perks and lower core stats.

## The Eclipse: Dynamic Difficulty Scaling

The **saros number of levels** is further complicated by the "Eclipse" mechanic. During your journey as Arjun, you will encounter devices that trigger an atmospheric eclipse. This event warps the current biome, significantly increasing the difficulty but also the rewards.

When an Eclipse is active:
*   Enemies become more aggressive and gain new attack patterns.
*   Special "Unblockable" projectiles are introduced that can reduce your maximum health.
*   The yield of currency (used for meta-progression) is greatly increased.
*   You gain access to specific "Power Weapon" charges more quickly by blocking standard projectiles.

Activating the Eclipse is often mandatory to unlock the path to a biome's final boss, but once you have defeated that boss once, the Eclipse becomes an optional high-risk, high-reward choice for future runs.

## Combat Fundamentals and Gear Scaling

To survive the six biomes that make up the **saros number of levels**, you must master the synergy between your gear and your core stats. In Saros, your character has three primary attributes: **Health, Shields, and Power**.

Unlike many roguelites where loot is entirely random, Saros features a scaling system where specific weapons perform better based on your stats. For example, certain Hand Cannons might deal bonus "Weak Point" damage that scales directly with your current Max Health. This allows you to "build" Arjun during a run to favor a specific playstyle, whether that be a tanky front-liner or a glass-cannon power-user.

### Weapon Perks and Proficiency
As you progress through the levels, your Proficiency level increases. Higher Proficiency allows weapons to drop with more perks, up to a maximum of five. These perks can fundamentally change how a weapon behaves:
1.  **Ricochet:** Bullets bounce off surfaces to hit hidden enemies.
2.  **Homing:** Projectiles track targets automatically.
3.  **Shield-Eater:** Deals massive damage to enemy barriers but reduces fire rate.
4.  **Adrenaline Link:** Increases damage the longer you go without taking a hit.

> **Warning:** Some high-tier perks disable the game's auto-aim feature in exchange for massive damage. Only use these if you are confident in your manual tracking skills.
