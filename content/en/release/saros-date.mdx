## Projectiles as Opportunities: Reimagined Combat

Unlike traditional roguelikes where the goal is simply to avoid incoming fire, Saros encourages players to interact with danger. The creative direction for this title focuses on making projectiles a resource rather than just an obstacle course. By utilizing the Sultari shield and the power weapon system, you can convert defensive maneuvers into devastating offensive counterattacks.

### The Sultari Shield and Parrying
The shield is not just a safety net; it is a weapon. Holding the shield button allows you to absorb specific blue projectiles to charge your power weapon. However, the high-skill ceiling lies in the **Parry** mechanic. By timing your shield activation at the precise moment of impact, you can reflect red projectiles back at turrets or stagger elite enemies, opening them up for massive critical damage.

### Corruption and Yellow Bullets
The most dangerous threats in Saros often come in the form of yellow "corrupted" bullets. These deal direct damage to your max integrity (health) and impose persistent debuffs. Early in the game, you unlock the ability to cleanse this corruption by activating your power weapon. This creates a high-risk, high-reward loop where you must intentionally stay near danger to maintain your offensive capabilities.

> **⚠️ Warning:** The Sultari shield has a limited duration and a cooldown period. Relying too heavily on passive blocking will eventually leave you vulnerable to the more complex bullet patterns of elite bosses.

## Progression: The Armor Matrix and Hian

Permanent progression is a core pillar of the Saros experience. Through the Armor Matrix, players can invest a currency called Lucenite to unlock buffs that persist across deaths. These can range from increased health pools to entirely new suit abilities.

Another rare resource, **Hian**, is used for armor alteration. While Lucenite focuses on raw stats, Hian is typically reserved for specialized upgrades like the parry ability or enhancing the efficiency of the power weapon. Because Hian is available in limited quantities within each biome, players must choose their upgrades carefully based on their preferred playstyle.

### Agency and Difficulty
For veterans who find the permanent upgrades too powerful, Housemark has included a "Hardcore Purist" toggle. You can turn off most permanent unlocks if you want to experience the game as a pure test of skill. This provides a level of agency rarely seen in the genre, allowing you to tailor the challenge to your own expertise.

## The Eclipse: Mandated Suffering

One of the most unique features players will encounter after the **saros date** is the Eclipse system. The Eclipse acts as a global modifier that shifts the environment into a high-danger state. During an Eclipse, enemies become significantly more aggressive, and their bullet patterns gain additional layers of complexity.

In the first two biomes, players are forced to interact with the Eclipse as part of the narrative progression. However, in later stages, you may gain the ability to control when the Eclipse activates. This adds a strategic layer to every run: do you trigger the Eclipse for better loot drops and more Lucenite, or do you play it safe to ensure you reach the next boss?

> **💡 Tip:** Always keep an eye on your Power Weapon charge before an Eclipse starts. You will need the cleansing effect of the Power Weapon to survive the inevitable corruption damage from yellow bullets.
