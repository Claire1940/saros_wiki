Surviving the hostile environments of Carcosa requires more than just quick reflexes; it demands a deep understanding of the **saros skill tree** and the permanent progression systems integrated into the game. Unlike its predecessor, Returnal, Housemarque’s latest shooter introduces a robust hub known as "The Passage," where players can invest resources to ensure each subsequent run is more powerful than the last. At the heart of this progression is a machine interface called Primary, which serves as the central terminal for your upgrades.

Whether you are looking to increase your survivability through the Armor Matrix or want to manipulate the difficulty using Carcosen modifiers, mastering the **saros skill tree** is essential for reaching the later biomes. In this guide, we will break down every component of the Primary system, from the core branches of the skill tree to the intricate balancing act of risk-reward modifiers that define the high-stakes gameplay of Saros.

## The Primary System: Your Hub for Permanent Progression

Primary is more than just a menu; it is the "Voice of Sultari," a companion and progression hub that Arjun accesses within the safety of The Passage. While Saros maintains its roguelike roots by resetting certain elements upon death, the Primary system allows for "Comeback Stronger" features that remain with you permanently.

The interface is divided into three distinct sections that players must manage:
1.  **The Armor Matrix:** The traditional skill tree where you spend currency on permanent stat boosts.
2.  **Carcosen Modifiers:** A unique risk-reward system that lets you toggle buffs and debuffs.
3.  **The World Dial:** A navigation and lore tracking system for the various biomes of Carcosa.

> **💡 Tip:** Always visit Primary immediately after returning to The Passage. Spending your accumulated Lucinites before starting a new cycle is the only way to ensure you don't lose your purchasing power.

## The Armor Matrix: Navigating the Saros Skill Tree

The Armor Matrix represents the actual **saros skill tree**. It functions as a branching path of upgrades that enhance Arjun’s combat effectiveness and survivability. To progress through the tree, players must first unlock the foundational node: **Second Chance**.

### Core Branches of the Armor Matrix

Once Second Chance is acquired, the tree splits into three primary disciplines. Each branch focuses on a specific aspect of the gameplay loop, allowing players to specialize their build based on their preferred playstyle.

| Branch Name | Primary Focus | Key Benefits |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Resilience** | Defensive Stats | Increases maximum armor integrity and damage resistance. |
| **Command** | Offensive Utility | Enhances shield capacity and improves power weapon efficiency. |
| **Drive** | Resource Acquisition | Boosts the rate of Lucinite gains from fallen enemies. |

### The Importance of Second Chance
The Second Chance node is arguably the most important upgrade in the early game. For a cost of 100 Lucinites, it grants Arjun the ability to ground pound and revive instantly upon his first death in a cycle. Crucially, this ability also purges any "Corruption" buildup, resetting your armor integrity to its maximum state and allowing you to continue the fight without restarting from the biome's beginning.

## Resources and Currencies: Lucinites and Hian

To climb the **saros skill tree**, you need to gather specific resources found within the biomes. There are two primary currencies displayed in the UI that dictate what you can unlock and when.

*   **Lucinites (Yellow L Symbol):** These are the primary monetary units in Saros. Similar to Obolites in Returnal, they drop from defeated enemies and are used to purchase nodes in the Armor Matrix.
*   **Hian (Purple Symbol):** A much rarer resource. While its exact function is tied to the game's deeper lore—possibly representing "joyful memories"—it appears to be a biome-specific collectible required for high-level progression or story-related unlocks.

| Resource | Rarity | Primary Use |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Lucinite** | Common | Buying Skill Tree nodes and mid-run items. |
| **Hian** | Rare | Unlocking advanced World Dial features and lore. |

## Carcosen Modifiers: The Risk-Reward Mechanics

One of the most innovative features of the **saros skill tree** ecosystem is the Carcosen Modifier system. This allows players to hand-select up to six "Protections" (buffs) and six "Trials" (debuffs) to customize their run. However, the system requires a strict balance. You cannot simply stack buffs to make the game easier; you must accept Trials to offset the power gained from Protections.

### Balancing the Scale
Each modifier has a point value. Protections carry negative values, while Trials carry positive values. To proceed with a run, the total sum of your selected modifiers must stay within a "grace window" (typically within 3 points of zero).

**Common Protections (Buffs):**
*   **Lucinite Magnetism:** Pulls currency toward Arjun from a distance.
*   **Damage Enhancement:** Increases base weapon damage output.
*   **Perfect Reload:** Widens the window for active reloads, granting instant ammo.
*   **Corruption Reduction:** Slows the rate at which corrupted projectiles degrade armor.

**Common Trials (Debuffs):**
*   **Corruption Enhancement:** Increases the severity of corruption damage.
*   **Lucinite Devaluation:** Reduces the amount of currency you can bring back to The Passage.
*   **Damage Degradation:** Lowers your overall weapon damage.

> **Warning:** Attempting to start a cycle with an unbalanced modifier scale will lock the Primary interface. Always ensure your Trials provide enough "weight" to support your Protections.

## The World Dial: Biome Navigation and Lore

The final piece of the progression puzzle is the World Dial. This section of Primary tracks your progress across the five confirmed biomes of Carcosa, such as the **Shattered Rise** and the **Ancient Depths**. 

The World Dial serves several functions:
1.  **Fast Travel:** Allows Arjun to move between unlocked biomes quickly.
2.  **Objective Tracking:** Highlights main missions, such as locating Sultari enforcers or defeating bosses like Bastion.
3.  **Lore Collection:** Tracks Audio Logs, Text Logs, and "Hollow Logs" (holographic cutscenes) discovered in each region.

### Biome Data Overview

| Biome | Key Feature | Lore Context |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Shattered Rise** | Red Fauna / Gray Stone | Location of the missing Sultari colony. |
| **Ancient Depths** | Mining Operations | A subterranean region where Lucinites were heavily harvested. |

## Proficiency and Weapon Scaling

While the **saros skill tree** handles your physical attributes, your offensive power is largely tied to your Proficiency level. Visible on the HUD, Proficiency dictates the quality and level of weapons you find during a run. 

While it remains to be seen if Proficiency resets entirely upon death as it did in previous Housemarque titles, the Armor Matrix does offer "Attribute Growth" nodes that provide bonuses to your branches as your Proficiency increases. This creates a synergy between your permanent skills and your performance within a single cycle.

## FAQ

**Q: Is the saros skill tree reset every time I die?**

A: No. The Armor Matrix within the Primary system is a permanent progression mechanic. Once you purchase a node using Lucinites, those benefits stay with Arjun across all future cycles.

**Q: How do I unlock the Second Chance ability?**

A: Second Chance is the first node in the Armor Matrix. It costs 100 Lucinites and must be purchased at the Primary terminal in The Passage before you can progress further into the Resilience, Command, or Drive branches.

**Q: What happens if I don't balance my Carcosen Modifiers?**

A: If the scale is unbalanced (too many Protections without enough Trials), the Primary system will not allow you to finalize your loadout. You must maintain a balance within the allowed grace window to benefit from any buffs during your run.

**Q: Can I change my skills mid-run?**

A: Permanent skills in the Armor Matrix cannot be changed mid-run, but Carcosen Modifiers can be adjusted whenever you return to The Passage between cycles. Items found during the run (similar to parasites) may provide temporary changes, but the core tree is managed at the hub.
