## Mastering the Eclipse Mechanic

The defining feature of Saros is the Eclipse. Every biome begins under "Uninterrupted Light," offering a relatively stable environment. However, to progress or claim the best rewards, players must eventually trigger the Eclipse. This transforms the level in real-time, altering both the environment and the threats within.

| Level State | Enemy Difficulty | Reward Quality | Environmental Hazards |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Standard Light** | Moderate | Standard Currency | Minimal |
| **Eclipse State** | High (Ferocious) | High (Corrupted Gear) | High (Poison Swamps/Traps) |

Under the Eclipse, enemies drop significantly more currency, but they also inflict **Corruption Damage**. This semi-permanent debuff reduces your maximum HP until you can clear it by killing specific enemies or finding rare purifiers. It creates a high-stakes risk/reward loop that keeps every run feeling fresh and dangerous.

## Meta-Progression and the Skill Tree

One of the most praised aspects of Saros—and a reason the **saros steam release** is so anticipated—is its robust meta-progression. Housemarque has moved away from the rigid difficulty of their past titles, offering players a "cushioned floor" that rises as they play.

The Skill Tree allows players to spend currency earned during runs on permanent stat upgrades. This means that even a "failed" run contributes to Arjun's overall power.
1.  **Stat Nodes:** Increase base health, damage, and shield efficiency.
2.  **Ability Unlocks:** New movement techniques and permanent Metroidvania-style tools (like the charged melee).
3.  **Shortcut Systems:** Once a biome is cleared, you can instantly teleport back to it on future runs, removing the need for tedious repetition.

> **Warning:** While the skill tree makes the game more accessible, skipping the "farming" phase and rushing into later biomes like the mechanical sewers will result in a steep difficulty spike. Balance your progression by revisiting earlier biomes to "get fat" with stats.

## Combat Mechanics: Parry, Shield, and Projectiles

The combat in Saros is a "chessboard" of projectile management. Players must distinguish between different colored threats and react accordingly using the new defensive suite.

*   **Blue Projectiles:** These can be absorbed by your **Shield**. Absorbing these converts the energy into ammo for your Power Attack.
*   **Yellow Projectiles:** Exclusive to the Eclipse state. These apply Corruption and must be dodged or outmaneuvered.
*   **Red Projectiles:** These are "unblockable" by standard means. They cannot be shielded or dashed through safely. Instead, they must be **Parried** using the R1/Bumper timing to reflect damage and stagger the attacker.

Every enemy has a **Stagger Meter**. By consistently landing hits or parrying red projectiles, you can leave even the most massive "Alpha" enemies vulnerable to high-damage finishers. This system ensures that combat remains strategic rather than just a test of twitch reflexes.
