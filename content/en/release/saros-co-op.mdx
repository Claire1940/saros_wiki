## Mastering the Eclipse Together

The Eclipse is a game-changing mechanic that corrupts the world of Carcosa. It escalates the audio, visuals, and gameplay difficulty to extreme levels. In a cooperative setting, the Eclipse requires constant communication. Hostiles will begin firing corrupted projectiles that reduce your maximum armor integrity. If both players are hit simultaneously, your run can end in seconds.

To counter this, players must utilize their power weapons effectively. Successfully landing hits with power weapons can restore lost armor integrity, making it vital to coordinate who is attacking which corrupted foe. Furthermore, weapons and artifacts themselves become corrupted during this phase. While this adds negative modifiers, it also provides significant power boosts that a well-coordinated team can exploit to clear rooms faster.

## Permanent Progression and the "Primary" Hub

All cooperative efforts lead back to "Primary," the voice of Soltari. This central hub is where you engage with the game's permanent progression systems. Even if a run ends in failure, the resources gathered during your **saros co op** sessions can be used to upgrade your armor's attributes and acquire special perks.

> **💡 Tip:** Always prioritize "Armor Integrity" and "Power Weapon Recharge" upgrades in the early game to sustain longer runs during the Eclipse phases.

The Primary hub also allows you to teleport directly to any biome you have previously unlocked. This is a massive quality-of-life feature for multiplayer teams, as it allows you to skip earlier, easier sections and jump straight into the challenge where you left off. This ensures that your limited gaming sessions are spent on meaningful progression rather than retreading old ground.

## Customizing the Challenge: Carcosan Modifiers

One of the most unique aspects of the game is the ability to adjust the experience through Carcosan modifiers. These can be accessed via Primary and allow you to fine-tune the difficulty to suit your team's skill level. However, there is a catch: all adjustments must be kept in balance. If you make hostiles weaker, you may have to sacrifice your "second chance" ability or reduce the drop rate of high-tier artifacts.

| Modifier Type | Effect on Gameplay | Team Strategy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Hostile Strength** | Increases/Decreases enemy HP and damage. | Lower this for learning patterns; raise it for better loot. |
| **Second Chance** | Toggles the ability to revive once per run. | Disabling this requires extremely cautious play. |
| **Corruption Rate** | Speeds up the onset of the Eclipse. | High risk, high reward for experienced teams. |
| **Loot Quality** | Increases the rarity of found artifacts. | Usually requires a trade-off in enemy difficulty. |

## Tactical Combat and Biome Traversal

In **saros co op**, movement is just as important as shooting. Carcosa is a shapeshifting planet, meaning the layout of biomes can change between runs. Staying close to your partner is generally recommended, but spreading out can sometimes be beneficial to avoid being caught in a single flurry of corrupted projectiles.

When you encounter an alien device that calls the Eclipse, make sure both players are prepared. Ensure your power weapons are fully charged and your current artifacts are optimized. Because weapons can change behavior when corrupted, you should quickly communicate any changes in your loadout to your partner. If your weapon suddenly gains an area-of-effect blast but loses fire rate, your partner may need to pick up the slack for single-target DPS.

### Key Combat Strategies for Teams:
1.  **Crossfire Positioning:** Position yourselves on opposite sides of large hostiles to split their attention and expose weak points.
2.  **Resource Management:** Don't grab every health or armor pickup immediately. Communicate who has the lowest integrity to maximize the team's longevity.
3.  **Power Weapon Cycling:** Stagger the use of your power weapons to ensure at least one player always has a way to restore armor integrity during the Eclipse.

## FAQ

**Q: Does Saros co op support cross-platform play?**

A: As of the April 30, 2026 launch, the game is a PlayStation 5 exclusive. Therefore, multiplayer is currently limited to the PlayStation Network ecosystem.

**Q: How does the "Come Back Stronger" mechanic work in a team?**

A: While individual runs end upon death (unless a second chance modifier is active), the resources you collect are used at the Primary hub to permanently upgrade your character. In **saros co op**, both players earn progression currency, allowing both to grow stronger regardless of who landed the final blow on a boss.

**Q: Can we play the entire story in Saros co op?**

A: Yes, the game is designed to be fully playable from start to finish in cooperative mode. The narrative involving Echelon Four and the mystery of Carcosa unfolds for both players as they progress through the biomes.

**Q: What happens to my loot if my partner disconnects?**

A: The game features robust session saving. If a connection is lost, most progression earned up to the last checkpoint or biome transition is typically retained, though it is always best to ensure a stable internet connection before starting a deep run into the Eclipse.
